How this tour maps to the product

The carousel below mirrors the first-time onboarding you would see after installing Thought Sweep on iOS: capture, organize, build a digital memory, remember people, swipe through recall, and tier your to-dos. Each slide pairs a headline with a single clarifying line so you can scan fast, then dive into the real app for depth.

Use this page when you need a visual checklist of what the app emphasizes—speed first, structure second—without downloading yet. When you are ready, jump to Download or try the interactive flow on the Demo page.

Nothing on this page writes to your device beyond ordinary browser caching; the slides are static HTML and the “Next” control only swaps visibility in the page.

Feature summary

Quick reference for what each onboarding moment highlights versus what ships in the full mobile experience.

Capture & triage
Here: hero art plus a reminder to sort now or later. In app: one-tap capture, optional voice, and smart prompts tuned for mobile.
Organization
Here: explains uncategorized thoughts, to-dos, and working memory. In app: drag-and-drop style moves between real categories and filters.
Digital memory & people
Here: positions Thought Sweep as a personal knowledge base and rolodex of ideas tied to people. In app: richer cards, dates, and search across entries.
Recall & to-dos
Here: swipe metaphor and priority tiers described in one line each. In app: full-screen review gestures plus notifications where you enable them.

Tips after onboarding

  • Finish the carousel once, then revisit any slide using the dots—order matches the install story.
  • Pair categories with a simple naming scheme (work, personal, health) before importing older notes.
  • Export or back up from the real app periodically; this marketing site does not sync your library.

Web experience vs. iOS app

This website previews visuals and copy in the browser. Thought Sweep itself is an iOS app with native performance, offline-friendly capture where supported, and App Store updates. Treat the web pages as orientation; treat the installed app as the source of truth for features and privacy settings.
